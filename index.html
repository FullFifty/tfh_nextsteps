The growth picture

Connect visits more than doubled from 2023 to 2024, then kept climbing.

The jump from 446 to 982 between 2023 and 2024 is the most significant single-year shift in the dataset — a 120% increase in first-step engagement. 2025 added another 14% on top of that, reaching 1,119.

Growth is no longer the constraint. The question is what happens after someone walks through the door.

03

Engagement is up. Conversion is down.

The paradox

More people are taking classes than ever — yet fewer are becoming members.

Essentials and Discover are not sequential gates — they're parallel engagement tracks. Combined class starts have grown from 407 in 2023 to 738 in 2025, an 81% increase.

But the ratio of class engagement to membership has broken down. In 2023, 407 class starts produced 172 members (2.4:1). In 2025, 738 class starts produced 124 members (5.9:1). The classes are working as engagement tools. Something downstream is not closing.

Class engagement vs. members made — by year
The timing pattern

Members are made in cohort cycles — roughly every 6–8 weeks.

The monthly member data shows a consistent alternating pattern across all three years: high months, near-zero months, high months again. This is the signature of scheduled class cohorts, not continuous enrollment.

The open question: what happens to people who visit Connect but miss the next cohort window? If the gap between Connect and the next class start is longer than 4–6 weeks, the data suggests a meaningful dropout risk.

04

Baptism: milestone, not metric

Baptism is tracked separately because it doesn't map to the discipleship pathway — it's a spiritual milestone that can happen at any point. The weekly baseline has been stable at 3–5 across all three years. What inflates the annual totals are large-group events.

05

What the data says

01
Top-of-funnel is healthy and growing
Connect visits have increased 151% in three years. The church is not struggling to attract first-time visitors. The awareness and welcome experience is working.
02
Membership conversion is in a three-year decline
38.6% → 19.4% → 11.1%. Each year, a smaller fraction of Connect visitors becomes members. This is the most important number in the dataset and the one that needs a strategic response.
03
Classes engage but don't close
Essentials and Discover participation is up 81% since 2023. Yet membership is down. The classes are creating engagement. Something in the final commitment step — the ask, the timing, or the pathway — is breaking down.
04
Cohort gaps may be losing people
The alternating monthly member pattern confirms class-based cohorts launching every 6–8 weeks. Someone who visits Connect in a "dead" month has to wait up to two months for the next on-ramp. That gap is a risk.
05
Baptism is a separate story
2025's 754 baptisms look dramatic but are driven by two large-group events. The weekly baseline (~4/week) has not changed meaningfully. Report them separately to avoid distorting the discipleship picture.
06
The open question
Why is conversion declining as engagement grows? The data raises the question but can't answer it. Is the Connect visitor profile changing? Is the membership ask unclear? Is the cohort wait losing people? This is what to investigate next.
